Hahow APP redesign

As an online learning enthusiast, I often evaluate educational apps. I redesigned one of them, Hahow, a mobile learning app, based on user research and usability test findings.

Major usability issues

Hahow has its own video player to diplay their courses. Most of the controls on the player will be familiar to you if you have watched online videos before. However, the Hahow video player still lacks some features that users need.

Speed control

Some courses would be too difficult or too easy to some students and would need a speed control button to play slower or faster. However, it is not enabled in Hahow.

Resolution

Hahow decided to let the APP detect users' device and value internet connection environment, choosing the best resolution itself while playing the videos. However, sometimes the detection is not accurate or users are willing to wait some more time for high resolution. In this case, users still need the resolution control button to make the video play in the right resolution.

The nextÂ course

When a user finishes a course in full-screen mode and wants to play the next one, the user needs to turn off the full screen, choose the next course from the playlist, and turn on the full screen again.

Layout

During play, the play/pause, rewind/fast forward buttons and playback bar stay right in the bottom of the video. These controls and the half-transparent mask under them block users' view to subtitles. Users need subtitles to help them to decide when to stop rewind/fast. In addtion, users couldn't hide the control panel right after the control had shown by clicking the blank space.

Recommendations

  • make the control panel full screen
  • lower opacity of the control panel background
  • add speed and resolution controls
  • add next course and playlist button
  • zoom the play/pause, rewind/fast buttons

    Scoping

    As an illustration of my recommendation, I decided to start from the player because it is the central part of Hahow, and that redesigning it will help with the most usability issue.

    Sketching out possibilities

    I started from sketching out the wireframes and possible interactions, and evaluated each solution based on my understanding of the challenge.

    Before and after

    After redesign, it's a lot more easier to click play/pause, rewind/fast forward buttons. The background of the control panel has a lower opacity so that the users can see the subtitles through the panel background.

    Research

    I generated interaction maps, and conducted interviews, comparative analysis, heuristic evaluation, usability testings, and collected quantitative survey data. Below is a summary of the methods I used.

    Interaction map

    To gain a comprehensive understanding of the app flow, I first generated an interaction map to walk through all the interactions.

    Interviews

    I conducted interviews with 3 users to understand user needs and the context of use.

    Google reviews

    I collected a total of 70 google play reviews, 43 reviews valid, to understand users' opinions on the use and effect.

    Comparative analysis

    To understand different desin patterns of players, I analyzed mainstream player in the world. This step served as an inspiration in my redesign of the app.

    Heuristic evaluation

    Based on Nielsen's 10 usability heuristic rules, we consolidated 7 major usability findings.

    Usability testing

    To further validate the usability issues that prevent users from completing the core learning functions, we planned a set of tasks involving featured functions that have revealed potential usability problems in previous studies.
